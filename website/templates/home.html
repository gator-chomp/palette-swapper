Understanding the Different Types of Colorblindness

Colorblindness is a condition in which individuals perceive color differently than the majority of the world, often lacking the ability to see certain colors, or even all colors around them. It is a very unique and rare condition and can impact people’s daily lives

What are the Different Kinds of Colorblindness?

Black and White Colorblindness (Achromatopsia): Sees the world in shades of gray and is usually a condition which is congenital and is present from birth.
Green Color Blindness (Deuteranopia): Do not have or have impaired green cone cells in their eyes which results in difficulty distinguishing between shades of green and red.
Red Color Blindness (Protanopia): The absence or malfunctioning of red cone cells causes the struggle to differentiate between red and green colors as they are seen as the same color.

Causes of the Different Types of Colorblindness

Black and White Colorblindness (Achromatopsia): This type of colorblindness is usually caused by cone cells in the retina being genetically mutated and since these cone cells are responsible for how color is perceived, it affects the person’s color vision. Furthermore, often cones are extremely impaired or even nonfunctional as well as can be often inherited only when both parents carry the mutated gene.
Green Color Blindness (Deuteranopia): The type of colorblindness is affected by the genetic mutation of the X chromosome and thus is why mostly men are affected by green-red colorblindness since they only carry one X chromosome. Women are able to compensate for the mutation of one of their X chromosomes with the other.
Red Color Blindness (Protanopia): The type of colorblindness is affected by the genetic mutation of the X chromosome and thus is why mostly men are affected by green-red colorblindness since they only carry one X chromosome. Women are able to compensate for the mutation of one of their X chromosomes with the other.

Impact on Daily Life

Black and White Colorblindness: This type of colorblindness can often cause individuals to become extremely sensitive to light and can often lead to painful situations if they are in bright environments. Furthermore, some people might even have a hard time differentiating between small details as they have reduced visual acuity. This will make it hard for them to see fine details or even read small text. Lastly, the most obvious impact on the lives of Black and White colorblind people is when they have to perform tasks based on color discrimination. For example, if at work their boss tells them to bring the “blue” folder, they might have a hard time performing the simple task.
Green and Red Colorblindness: One of the main impacts of green and red colorblindness is driving as it is hard to differentiate between the traffic lights go and stop and can lead to dangerous situations. Furthermore, many professional settings have the default red-green combination for tasks such as graphic designing, electrical work, medical care, etc. which can lead to very difficult time for individuals with these conditions to go into these types of professions. Overall, since society uses red and green a lot for indicators, whether it be in sports, work situations, games, etc. life overall is made a bit more difficult and stressful.

Coping Strategies and Support

1. Wearing appropriate Eyewear: Using tinted or polarized lenses can often help individuals to reduce their sensitivity in bright environments as well as can sometimes help with their vision.

2. Assistive Technologies: New specialized apps have been made to help individuals with color blindness identify different colors and differentiate between colors which might come off as the same. These apps can help in daily tasks such as reading color coded text, helping color code outfits and colors of socks, etc.

3. Educational Awareness: It is highly important for people with colorblindness to help educate those around them and gather support in daily situations. Not speaking of the condition might lead to confusion and misunderstanding between family and friends and can often help in building an inclusive and accommodating environment.

4. Regular Eye Exams and Professional Help: It can often be helpful to regularly get eyes checked and monitor the state of the color vision. This can help detect any changes in the color vision of the individual and also help them find new ways to manage their conditions.
